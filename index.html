The problem

Solo family law attorneys lose $4,000 retainers because the follow-up email goes out tomorrow.

01

You finish a 60-minute family law consultation knowing exactly what the case needs. Then you spend the next hour writing the engagement letter manually, formatting the retainer agreement, and copying a LawPay link into a follow-up email — by which time the prospective client has called another attorney who got back to them faster.

02

A $4,000 divorce retainer is your single most important conversion. The window to close it is the 2 hours after consultation — when the client is still emotionally engaged and you are still fresh in their mind. Every hour of delay cuts conversion by measurable double digits.

03

Your billing software was built for billing, not for post-consultation conversion. There is no single tool that handles engagement letter generation, e-signature, and retainer collection in one step. So you stitch together DocuSign, LawPay, and your email client manually every single time.

How it works

Consultation ends. Engagement letter signs. Retainer lands. Done.

Step 1

Select case type, set retainer amount

During or immediately after consultation, select the case type and set the retainer amount. RetainerFlow pulls your engagement letter template and populates it with client details from your intake — no copy-paste, no manual merge.

Step 2

Engagement letter routes for e-signature instantly

RetainerFlow sends the prepopulated engagement letter to your client's email for e-signature — no separate DocuSign account required. Most clients sign within the hour while the consultation is still top of mind.

Step 3

LawPay retainer collected on signature

When the engagement letter is signed, RetainerFlow automatically sends the LawPay retainer payment request for the exact amount you specified. Your client pays in one step. You receive a notification when funds are confirmed.

The problem

Retainers are a blindspot for most law firms

You know what happened last month. You don't know what's in trust accounts until the end of the month. By then, a retainer has run dry and you've already spent unbilled hours on it.

Retainers Run Dry Silently

You track time. You bill hours. But the retainer balance lives in a separate spreadsheet nobody updates until month-end. Surprise: you've already overspent.

Trust Accounting Is a Minefield

IOLTA rules are strict. Spreadsheets don't reconcile automatically. One wrong entry, and your trust account is out of balance. Your bank statements don't match. Nobody knows why.

Billing Takes Hours You Could Bill

Every invoice requires: check the retainer balance, verify it's not depleted, calculate the invoice amount, post the payment, update the balance sheet. By hand. Every time.

How it works

Three things. Real-time. Compliant.

01

Track

Your time entries sync to retainer balances. As soon as you log an hour in Clio (or your time tracker), the retainer balance updates automatically. No manual entry.

Real-time sync

02

Bill

One click generates an invoice, deducts from the retainer, and posts to your practice management system. The retainer balance drops. Ready to collect new prepayment.

One-click invoicing

03

Comply

Trust account reconciles automatically. Ledger balances match bank statements. Audit logs capture every transaction. IOLTA rules are built in, not optional.

IOLTA-ready
